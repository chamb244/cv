    \section{RESEARCH PROFILE}



        
        \begin{onecolentry}
        Within CIMMYT’s Sustainable Agrifood System Program, I lead a global research team that evaluates trends, drivers and impacts of rural transformation in developing countries. A major thread of this work is focused on the evaluation of extension methods and information technology innovations for guiding on-farm investments and technology change in smallholder systems. This work uses rigorous ex post approaches to evaluating piloted interventions (e.g., randomized control trials), as well as spatially-explicit ex ante modeling work that integrates agronomy, soil science and economics to inform scaling strategies over time and space. Other research activities include work to understand how rapid evolution of rural land, labor and financial services markets are changing the incentives and enabling conditions for on-farm technology change in developing countries. More broadly, my research interests focus on how policies and investments can best influence rural development trajectories towards desired social, economic and environmental outcomes.
        \end{onecolentry}
